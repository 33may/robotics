description: In presentations/pitches, don't claim capability directly — demonstrate it with evidence (artifacts, diagrams, screenshots)
metadata:

In presentation and pitch material, do NOT write direct self-claims like "this proves I'm capable" or "I can manage the project." Let evidence carry it — artifacts, diagrams, screenshots, results.

**Why:** Anton's stance for the Albert/VBTI humanoid pitch — a CEO audience trusts shown evidence over stated competence. Explicit capability claims read as telling, not proving.

**How to apply:** When drafting slides, meeting docs, or any persuasive material, frame sections around concrete evidence and let the reader infer the conclusion. Strip editorial lines that assert competence. Mark where real artifacts (videos/diagrams/screenshots) go instead of describing how good the work is.
