01Live robot policy demo
Why we are building this

Robots need to adapt faster

Classical automation is powerful, but every variation costs engineering time. Modern robot learning aims to transfer more of that effort into data and models.

1

Programming is brittle

Small changes in objects, positions or lighting can break carefully scripted behavior.

2

Demonstrations are natural

A human can show the behavior directly through teleoperation, producing reusable robot data.

3

Models can generalize

Vision‑Language‑Action policies connect perception, task intent and continuous control.

02Problem framing
What is running

SmolVLA controls the SO‑101 arm

The policy receives images, robot state and a language prompt, then predicts short action chunks for the arm.

VLA model concept
Inputs

Camera frames + joint state + task text, for example “pick up the cup”.

Outputs

Continuous robot actions executed asynchronously on the SO‑101 follower arm.

04Engineering pipeline
What to look for in the demo

Success is measured behavior, not just movement

The important question: does the learned policy reliably complete the task under realistic variation?

Demo checklist

Start robot + cameras + inference process.

Give task instruction and observe action chunks.

Repeat with small scene changes.

Current research focus

Robustness to object position and camera variation.

Better evaluation loops before scaling to harder tasks.

Combining real demonstrations with simulation support.
