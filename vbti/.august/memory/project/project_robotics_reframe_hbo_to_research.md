The project started as an applied company deliverable, but the real contribution is broader: a complete robotics research pipeline from 3D scene reconstruction into a physics engine, real teleoperation data collection, simulation training, and simulation inference. The work then shifted from just using existing baselines to building and evaluating stronger VLA/IL baselines, because the original benchmark gap made comparison impossible. The current framing should emphasize that this is a research and engineering path inside a company context, not a narrow one-off task.

**Why:** The user wants the graduation story and table-meeting pitch to reflect the actual scope and learning: building the infrastructure, discovering the baseline gap, and moving toward stable, scalable robotics methods.

**How to apply:** When describing the project, lead with the full pipeline and research arc, then mention the duck-cup evaluation, dataset scaling, UVA next steps, and the possible transition to humanoid work as the next stage.
